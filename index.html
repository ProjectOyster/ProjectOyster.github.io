PROJECT OYSTER

1. The problem

Oceans, which cover 70% of our planet's surface, play a critical role in the health of our world and its inhabitants. Our oceans, however, are polluted. Disposable plastic products and styrofoam containers, for example, can have an immediate and indirect impact on marine life as well as humans. It has been estimated the yearly cost of invasive species to the US economy is $137 billion. This damage is amplified by climate change, which forces invasive species to the poles, resulting in an expanded area of intrusions. Current models of ocean current are limited in scale by the lack of data, and accuracy due to little scalability.

2. Our Technology

Project Oyster uses physics-informed neural networks, which combines real life physics constraints into neural networks. Since the motion of objects are governed by physics, we can study the way particles are carried through substances with physics-informed neural networks. Our project is the first to model ocean currents to alleviate the damage caused invasive species, which is an under-explored topic.
