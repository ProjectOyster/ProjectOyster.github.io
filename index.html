PROJECT OYSTER

The problem

Disposable plastic products and styrofoam containers can cause immediate or indirect impact on marine life and humans

It has been estimated the yearly cost of invasive species to the US economy is $137 billion.

Climate change amplifies damage forcing invasive species to the poles, resulting in an expanded area of intrusions.

Our Technology

Project Oyster uses physics-informed neural networks, which combines real life physics constraints into neural networks. There is a breakthrough in studying the way in which particles are carried through substances that could be used for our project.

Cost Efficient

Computationally Efficient

About Us

Project Oyster intends to protect fisheries for countries that rely on fish as a staple food source, thereby ensuring food security, while also preparing to preserve the marine ecology and prevent the extinction of existing species.
