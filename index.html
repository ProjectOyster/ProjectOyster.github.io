PROJECT OYSTER

1. The problem?

Oceans, which cover 70% of our planet's surface, play a critical role in the health of our world and its inhabitants. Our oceans, however, are polluted. Disposable plastic products and styrofoam containers, for example, can have an immediate and indirect impact on marine life as well as humans. It has been estimated the yearly cost of invasive species to the US economy is $137 billion. Made worse with climate change, which alters ocean current, resulting in more intrusions. Current models of ocean current are limited in scale by the lack of data.

2. Our Technology

Project Oyster uses physics-informed neural networks, which can combine real life physics constraints into the flexibility of neural networks. Since the motion of objects are governed by physics, we can study the way particles are carried through substances. we can model ocean currents, to identify the movement of invasive species, which is under-explored, and our project is the first to do so.

3. About Us

We are a group of enthusiastic secondary school students whom aim to solve problems regarding invasive species through AI.
